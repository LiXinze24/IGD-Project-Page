IGD: A Unified Generative Framework for Semantic-Driven Mechanical Design

The IGD Framework: A unified bioinspired architecture integrating historical design knowledge, multimodal design data, and generative modeling (LLMs & TNNs) into a scalable workflow for end-to-end mechanical design.

Abstract

Mechanical design integrates domain knowledge, analytical reasoning, and geometric modeling to transform functional requirements into feasible structures. Achieving this process in a truly end-to-end manner remains challenging, as current large language model (LLM) based approaches still lack three-dimensional (3D) spatial perception, awareness of physical and manufacturing constraints.

This work addresses the absence of a unified, scalable and knowledge-grounded intelligent framework for mechanical design by integrating historical design knowledge, multimodal design data and generative intelligence models into an intelligent generative design (IGD) paradigm. Here we demonstrate that the proposed IGD framework effectively integrates general LLMs, the supervised fine-tuned LLM and task-specific neural networks (TNNs) within a multi-agent architecture to perform intent-driven design tasks of functional analysis, 3D modeling, 3D assembly and kinematic reasoning, thereby enabling mechanical design that is directly driven by semantic requirements.

The framework covers design workflow and completes complex mechanical design tasks with high geometric and assembly accuracy and without human intervention. The findings indicate that semantic-level design intent can serve as a direct driver for the complete mechanical design workflow.

Methodology

The IGD framework employs a multi-agent architecture (Task Planning, Parts Design, Parts Modeling, Parts Assembly) coordinated by an Ant Colony Bionic Agents Collaboration (ACBAC) mechanism. This allows for dynamic task decomposition and specialized execution.

Iterative 3D Modeling (CRP & CGP)

We introduce Content-Retrieval Prompting (CRP) and Content-Generation Prompting (CGP) methods. The PM agent uses a dual-LLM refinement mechanism (Modeler + Checker) to ensure geometric accuracy and structural consistency in generated CADQuery code.

Graph-Based Assembly (GART)

The Graph-Based Assembly Relation Transfer (GART) method retrieves historical assembly subgraphs to guide the LLM. It infers mating constraints and spatial transformations, enabling the PA agent to assemble complex mechanisms with kinematic validity.

Results

1. Semantic-Driven Reducer Design

Given the instruction "design a 3D model of a standard first-stage cylindrical gear reducer...", IGD automatically generates the full set of 3D parts and a kinematically valid assembly. It outperforms standalone LLMs (e.g., GPT-4o, Gemini) which often produce geometric inconsistencies.


2. Design Optimization & Adaptation

IGD can perform adaptive redesign. For example, when improving a design for "low-speed heavy-load conditions," the framework automatically increases wall thickness, gear diameters, and shaft sizes while maintaining assembly constraints.
