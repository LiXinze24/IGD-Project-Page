IGD Framework: A unified generative paradigm for end-to-end mechanical design, driven by semantic intent and historical engineering knowledge.

Abstract

Mechanical design integrates domain knowledge, analytical reasoning, and geometric modeling to transform functional requirements into feasible structures. Achieving this process in a truly end-to-end manner remains challenging...

We present IGD, an intelligent generative design framework that integrates large language models (LLMs) and task-specific neural networks (TNNs) within a multi-agent architecture. Our framework demonstrates high geometric and assembly accuracy across complex mechanical tasks like reducer design and device optimization.

Methodology

Iterative 3D Modeling

Introducing CRP (Content-Retrieval Prompting) and CGP (Content-Generation Prompting) to ensure structural consistency and manufacturing feasibility.

Optimization Design

The Semantic-Driven IGD method retrieves design patterns to reason about kinematic constraints and spatial transformations for optimal mechanical structures.

Additional Design Results

Comprehensive results demonstrating IGD's capabilities in various mechanical design scenarios
